Timeful vs Whensdays

An honest comparison for picking a scheduling and availability tool - updated July 2026.

Timeful (formerly Schej) is a genuinely good availability poll: free, open-source, no login, and it can pull participants' Google or Outlook calendars straight into the grid. The hosted site is ad-supported, though, and where it stops is where the plan starts - Timeful finds the overlap, then hands the rest back to the group chat. Whensdays runs the same kind of poll and then runs the event too: RSVPs, the invite page, reminders, and the next occurrence.

The short verdict

Pick Timeful if you want an open-source overlap-finder - especially if everyone will connect a work calendar and all you need is the best meeting slot.

Pick Whensdays when the poll is for a real get-together: the winning times become scheduled dates with RSVPs, a shareable page, reminders people answer in one tap, and a group that plans the next one before the chat goes quiet.

Is Whensdays free like Timeful?

Yes - availability polls, RSVPs, reminders, groups, and calendar sync are all free.

Does Whensdays require an account like Timeful?

No. Guests vote and RSVP from the invite link with just a name - no login, same as Timeful.

What does Whensdays add on top of an availability poll?

The whole event: RSVPs and a guest list, a shareable event page with comments and photos, one-tap RSVP from email, automatic day-before reminders, scheduling several winning dates as one series, and groups that keep meeting - streaks, recaps, and a one-tap re-poll.
